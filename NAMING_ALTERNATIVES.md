## Top Recommendations

### #1: **Hirely** (hirely.io)
- Clean, modern, action-oriented
- "Hire" + "ly" suffix (friendly, SaaS-y)
- Easy to spell, easy to say
- Works for both sides ("I got hired on Hirely")

### #2: **Worksmith** (worksmith.io)
- Craftsman vibe
- Implies quality + skill
- Memorable, professional
- "Smith" = maker, expert

### #3: **Rivet** (userivet.com)
- Construction term (rivets hold things together)
- Short, punchy, brandable
- Visual logo potential
- "Riveting work" wordplay

### #4: **Taskfox** (taskfox.com)
- Animal mascot potential (fox = clever, fast)
- "Task" = clear what it does
- Friendly, approachable
- Great for marketing

### #5: **Bridgr** (bridgr.io)
- Exactly what you do (bridge clients + contractors)
- Modern spelling (drop the 'e')
- Clean, minimal
- Works internationally

---

## My Top Pick: **Hirely**

**Why:**
1. Clear value prop in the name
2. Modern SaaS naming convention
3. Works for both sides of the marketplace
4. Easy to remember and spell
5. Friendly, not corporate
6. Room to grow beyond contractors

**Logo concept:** Simple "H" with an upward arrow or checkmark
**Tagline:** "Find work. Keep 100%."
**Domain:** hirely.io or tryhirely.com

---

## Runner Up: **Rivet**

**Why:**
1. Construction/industry connection
2. Short, memorable, brandable
3. Strong visual identity potential
4. "Riveting" = exciting, engaging
5. Less generic than "hire" names

**Logo concept:** Rivet icon, industrial aesthetic
**Tagline:** "Connect. Build. Grow."
**Domain:** userivet.com or rivet.work

## How to Choose

1. **Say it out loud** — Does it sound good when you answer the phone?
2. **Spell test** — Can someone spell it after hearing it once?
3. **Logo test** — Can you visualize a strong logo?
4. **Domain test** — Is something reasonable available?
5. **Trademark check** — Search USPTO before committing
