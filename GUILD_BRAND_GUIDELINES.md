## Voice & Tone

### Guild Voice
- **Direct:** No fluff, no corporate speak
- **Inclusive:** Everyone's part of the guild
- **Empowering:** You control your work
- **Playful (subtle):** Anime/gaming references without being childish

### Tone Examples

**Instead of:** "We facilitate connections between service providers and consumers"
**Say:** "Post a quest. Find your party. Get it done."

**Instead of:** "Our platform enables seamless transactions"
**Say:** "No middleman. No BS. Just you and the work."

**Instead of:** "Contractor marketplace"
**Say:** "The guild for people who build things"

## Key Terminology

### Guild Terms (Use These)

| Old Term | Guild Term | Why |
|----------|------------|-----|
| Job Post | **Quest** | Anime/gaming vibe, adventure feel |
| Contractor | **Adventurer** or **Guild Member** | Hero vibe, skilled professional |
| Client | **Quest Giver** | They post the mission |
| Apply/Express Interest | **Apply to Quest** or **Join Party** | Action-oriented |
| Match | **Form Party** | Team formation |
| Job Complete | **Quest Complete** | Achievement unlocked |
| Platform | **Guild Hall** | The gathering place |

### When to Use What

**Public-facing (landing page, marketing):**
- Use "Quest" for jobs
- Use "Adventurer" for contractors (playful)
- Use "Guild Member" for contractors (professional)
- Use "Quest Giver" for clients

**Professional contexts (contracts, legal):**
- Use standard terms: job, contractor, client

**In-app (UI copy):**
- Mix based on context
- "Post a Quest" (button)
- "Browse Quests" (navigation)
- "Form Your Party" (match success)

## Visual Identity Notes

### Logo Direction
- **Icon:** Stylized guild hall, crossed tools, or overlapping shields
- **Style:** Clean, modern, slightly medieval/industrial
- **Colors:** Deep blue (#1e3a5f) + gold/amber (#f59e0b) for guild/fantasy feel

### Alternative: Modern Tech
- **Icon:** Geometric G, interconnected nodes
- **Style:** Minimal, SaaS aesthetic
- **Colors:** Navy + coral or teal

## Naming Conventions in Code/DB

**Database tables:**
- `quests` (not jobs)
- `adventurers` or `contractors` (not service_providers)
- `quest_givers` or `clients`
- `parties` or `matches`
- `guild_memberships` (subscriptions)

**API endpoints:**
- `/api/quests` (not /api/jobs)
- `/api/quests/:id/apply` (apply to quest)
- `/api/adventurers` (contractor profiles)

**Internal consistency:** Pick one set and stick to it.
